LOOK UP FROM YOUR PHONE

The video aims to raise awareness to the NYUAD community as a whole to “look up” from their phones and try to interact with others in the real life instead of being stuck in the virtual one. The aim of combining multiple clips of people scrolling endlessly through their phones is to give those people a wake-up call of this behavior that we are all guilty off but will be working on.

Project Description

This project investigates the NYUAD community's collective behavior in the excessive use of mobile phones and the virtual world. By emphasizing mundane campus life in the early morning, there is a noticeable pattern of people walking around with their phones only.

Process

The project was inspired by friends in the NYUAD community who refused to socialize in places like the dining hall (D2), The Palms, or even on their way to class. The student body has abused the use of mobile phones and social media, even when other friends are present in the same setting. The conundrum is a collective behavior that cannot be attributed to a single person. Dhabia, Luke, and I set out to different parts of campus to document the daily occurrence of this behavior. I combined the videos, edited them in IMovie, and then recorded a voice over for the video.
